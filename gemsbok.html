About

The gemsbok or gemsbuck (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope in the Oryx genus. It is native to the arid regions of Southern Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert.

The gemsbok is depicted on the coat of arms of Namibia, where the current population of the species is estimated at 373,000 individuals.

The name "gemsbok" in English is derived from Afrikaans, which itself is derived from the Dutch name of the male chamois. Although some superficial similarities in appearance are noticed, the chamois and the oryx are not closely related.
